# Product Analysis: Easy to Park

## Overview
**Easy to Park** is a peer-to-peer marketplace connecting individuals who have unused parking spaces (e.g., apartment residents, homeowners) with drivers who need short-term or long-term parking. It aims to solve urban parking scarcity by maximizing the use of existing private infrastructure.

## Target Audience
1. **Hosts (Space Owners):** Individuals living in residencies or apartments who own or rent a parking spot but do not currently use it. 
2. **Renters (Drivers):** Commuters, travelers, event attendees, or neighbors who struggle to find affordable, safe parking in congested areas.

## Core Value Proposition
- **For Hosts:** Passive income from an unused asset.
- **For Renters:** Cheaper, more convenient, and more secure parking options compared to commercial lots or street parking.

## Potential Features (For the Future)
- **User Verification:** To ensure safety, both hosts and renters would need their identities verified (e.g., ID checks, vehicle registration).
- **Interactive Map Search:** Renters can search for parking based on location, duration (hourly, daily, monthly), and vehicle size.
- **Listing Management:** Hosts can set custom availability calendars, pricing, and specific instructions (e.g., "spot #42 on the second basement level").
- **Secure Payments:** Integrated payment gateway holding funds in escrow until the parking period begins.
- **Messaging System:** In-app chat to coordinate access (especially for gated apartments).
- **Review System:** Two-way ratings to build trust within the community.

## Key Challenges to Keep in Mind
1. **Access Control:** Many apartment complexes are gated. Finding a seamless way for a renter to access a secure building (without compromising the building's overall security) will be a major design challenge.
2. **HOA and Lease Restrictions:** Many apartments or Homeowner Associations (HOAs) have rules against subletting parking spots. The platform may need to educate users on checking their lease agreements.
3. **Marketplace Bootstrapping:** Like Uber or Airbnb, it's a two-sided marketplace. You need enough parking spots to attract drivers, and enough drivers to make it worthwhile for hosts. Launching in a specific, dense neighborhood first is usually the best strategy.
